<p><b>Maybach Motorenbau</b> (<small>German pronun­cia­tion:</small> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_German" title="Help:IPA for German">[ˈmaɪ.bax]</a></span><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>) was a German car manufacturer. The company was founded in 1909 by <a href="/wiki/Wilhelm_Maybach" title="Wilhelm Maybach">Wilhelm Maybach</a> and his son, originally a <a href="/wiki/Subsidiary" title="Subsidiary">subsidiary</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Luftschiffbau_Zeppelin" title="Luftschiffbau Zeppelin">Luftschiffbau Zeppelin</a> <a href="/wiki/GmbH" class="mw-redirect" title="GmbH">GmbH</a></i> and was itself known as <i>Luftfahrzeug-Motorenbau GmbH</i> until 1912.</p>
<p>In 1960 Maybach was acquired by <a href="/wiki/Daimler-Benz" title="Daimler-Benz">Daimler-Benz</a> and remains nowadays as an <a href="/wiki/Luxury_vehicle#Ultra-luxury_cars" title="Luxury vehicle">ultra-luxury car</a> <a href="/wiki/Brand" title="Brand">brand</a> of <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz" title="Mercedes-Benz">Mercedes-Benz</a>, which is owned by <a href="/wiki/Daimler_AG" title="Daimler AG">Daimler AG</a>. Daimler currently produces an ultra-luxury edition of the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_S-Class" title="Mercedes-Benz S-Class">Mercedes-Benz S-Class</a> under the Mercedes-Maybach brand.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="1909.E2.80.9340:_Early_history">1909–40: Early history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maybach&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: 1909–40: Early history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Wilhelm Maybach was technical director of the <i><a href="/wiki/Daimler-Motoren-Gesellschaft" title="Daimler-Motoren-Gesellschaft">Daimler-Motoren-Gesellschaft</a></i> (DMG) until he left in 1907. On 23 March 1909 he founded the new company, <i>Luftfahrzeug-Motorenbau GmbH</i> (literally "Aircraft Engine Building Company"), with his son <a href="/w/index.php?title=Karl_Maybach&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Karl Maybach (page does not exist)">Karl Maybach</a> as director.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In 1912 they renamed it to <i>Maybach-Motorenbau GmbH</i> ("Maybach Engine Construction Company"). The company originally developed and manufactured diesel and petrol engines for <a href="/wiki/Zeppelin" title="Zeppelin">Zeppelins</a>, and then rail cars. Its <a href="/wiki/Maybach_Mb.IVa" title="Maybach Mb.IVa">Maybach Mb.IVa</a> was used in aircraft and airships of World War I.</p>
<p>The company first built an experimental car in 1919, introduced as a production model two years later at the Berlin Motor Show. Between 1921 and 1940, the company produced a variety of opulent vehicles, now regarded as classics. The company also continued to build heavy-duty diesel engines for marine and rail purposes.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="1940.E2.80.9345">1940–45</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maybach&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1940–45">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>During the Second World War, Maybach produced the engines for Nazi Germany's medium and heavy tanks, as well as the light tank, Panzer II. The engine plant was one of several industries <a href="/wiki/Bombing_of_Friedrichshafen_in_World_War_II" title="Bombing of Friedrichshafen in World War II">targeted at Friedrichshafen</a>.</p>
<p>After WW II the factory performed some repair work, but automotive production was never restarted, and some 20 years later, the company was renamed <a href="/wiki/MTU_Friedrichshafen" title="MTU Friedrichshafen">MTU Friedrichshafen</a>. <a href="/wiki/Daimler-Benz" title="Daimler-Benz">Daimler-Benz</a> purchased the company in 1960. Post-1960 the company was mainly used to make special editions of Mercedes cars in the W108 and W116 model range, which were virtually hand built. These cars however carried the Mercedes badge and serial numbers.</p>

<p>Daimler presented a luxury concept car at the 1997 Tokyo Motorshow. A production model based on it was introduced in two sizes&#160;– the <a href="/wiki/Maybach_57_and_62" title="Maybach 57 and 62">Maybach 57 and the Maybach 62</a>, reflecting the lengths of the automobiles in <a href="/wiki/Decimetres" class="mw-redirect" title="Decimetres">decimetres</a>. In 2005, the 57S was added, powered by a 6.0 L V12 <a href="/wiki/Bi-turbo" class="mw-redirect" title="Bi-turbo">bi-turbo</a> engine producing 450&#160;kW (603&#160;hp) and 1,000&#160;N·m (738&#160;lbf·ft) of torque, and featuring various cosmetic touches.</p>
<p>To promote the new Maybach line, Mercedes-Benz engaged figures such as Maybach heir <a href="/wiki/Ulrich_Schmid-Maybach" title="Ulrich Schmid-Maybach">Ulrich Schmid-Maybach</a> and golfer <a href="/wiki/Nick_Faldo" title="Nick Faldo">Nick Faldo</a>, to serve as <a href="/wiki/Brand_ambassador" title="Brand ambassador">brand ambassadors</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Initially, Daimler-Chrysler predicted annual sales of 2,000 worldwide with 50 per cent coming from the United States; however, these expectations never materialized.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In 2007, Mercedes bought back 29 US dealers, reducing the total from 71 to 42.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2010, only 157 Maybachs were sold worldwide, compared to 2,711 similarly priced Rolls-Royces.<sup id="cite_ref-money.cnn.com_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-money.cnn.com-7">[7]</a></sup> Just 3,000 have been sold worldwide since the brand was revived in 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Daimler announced in November 2011 that Maybach would cease to be a brand by 2013 and manufactured the last Maybach vehicle in December 2012. This was because of poor sales.<sup id="cite_ref-endofbrand_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-endofbrand-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-endofbrand1_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-endofbrand1-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cancellation">Cancellation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maybach&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Cancellation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>With poor sales expectations and the heavy impact of the <a href="/wiki/Financial_crisis_of_2007-08" class="mw-redirect" title="Financial crisis of 2007-08">2007-08 financial crisis</a>, Daimler AG undertook a complete review of the Maybach division,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> approaching <a href="/wiki/Aston_Martin" title="Aston Martin">Aston Martin</a> to engineer and style the next generation of Maybach models along with the next generation of <a href="/wiki/Lagonda" title="Lagonda">Lagondas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fuhrmans1_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fuhrmans1-12">[12]</a></sup> According to Automotive News, only 44 Maybachs had been sold in the U.S. through October 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>An article in <i><a href="/wiki/Fortune_(magazine)" title="Fortune (magazine)">Fortune</a></i> noted that Mercedes had missed out on the chance to purchase Rolls-Royce and Bentley when they were up for sale in the 1990s:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"Mercedes backpedaled and decided it needed to be in the ultra-luxury business too, but it went after it in a remarkably clumsy way."</p>
</blockquote>
<p>It further stated that the first Maybach models had poor driving dynamics compared to its contemporaries from Rolls-Royce and Bentley:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"Mercedes took an aging S-class chassis and plopped an absurdly elongated body on it&#160;... rather than develop a new car from the wheels up, as BMW did with Rolls-Royce, or cleverly use the underpinnings of an existing model like the <a href="/wiki/Volkswagen_Phaeton" title="Volkswagen Phaeton">Volkswagen Phaeton</a> for a new Bentley."</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Furthermore, Maybachs were never advertised as owner-driven vehicles, as the company believed that the luxury amenities would be sufficient to drive sales, and they even insisted that auto journalists (who usually test drive the vehicle) ride in the backseat.<sup id="cite_ref-money.cnn.com_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-money.cnn.com-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Another suggestion for Maybach's struggles was that parent Daimler had failed to differentiate it from its <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz" title="Mercedes-Benz">Mercedes-Benz</a> brand. While all three ultra-luxury marques share platforms and engines with other luxury brands from their parent auto company, Maybachs are built alongside the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_S-Class" title="Mercedes-Benz S-Class">Mercedes-Benz S-Class</a> flagship sedan, whereas <a href="/wiki/Rolls-Royce_Motor_Cars" title="Rolls-Royce Motor Cars">Rolls-Royce</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bentley" title="Bentley">Bentley</a> are assembled in England (separate from the rest of <a href="/wiki/BMW" title="BMW">BMW</a> and <a href="/wiki/Volkswagen_Group" title="Volkswagen Group">Volkswagen Group</a>'s production plants), and thus are regarded as being more "exclusive". Furthermore, the Maybach's pedigree was virtually unknown outside of Germany, unlike its British rivals which have long enjoyed renown worldwide;<sup id="cite_ref-fuhrmans1_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fuhrmans1-12">[12]</a></sup> indeed the 2006 <a href="/wiki/Rolls-Royce_Phantom_(2003)" title="Rolls-Royce Phantom (2003)">Rolls-Royce Phantom</a>'s interior evokes memories of a 1930s car while the Maybach 57S's inside makes no reference to its marque's history.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2011, Daimler's CEO Dieter Zetsche announced that the Maybach-brand would cease to exist in 2012, making room for other models of the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_S-Class" title="Mercedes-Benz S-Class">Mercedes-Benz S-Class</a>. The Maybach-limousines were still being sold up to the year 2013, but after that, the name "Maybach" would not be used anymore.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> On 14 August 2012, parent Daimler AG announced the official discontinuation of Maybach by releasing a price sheet officially discontinuing the Maybach 57, 57S, 62, 62S and Landaulet.<sup id="cite_ref-insideline.com_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-insideline.com-16">[16]</a></sup> On 17 December 2012, the last Maybach-vehicle was manufactured in Sindelfingen.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Revival">Revival</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maybach&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Revival">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The company announced that the line would be replaced by the next-generation of the Mercedes-Benz S-Class, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_S-Class_(W222)" title="Mercedes-Benz S-Class (W222)">Model W222</a>, due for the 2014 model year, particularly the <a href="/wiki/Wheelbase" title="Wheelbase">long wheelbase</a> S-Class Pullman. An executive told a Frankfurt newspaper that "Daimler came to the conclusion that the sales chances for the Mercedes brand were better than that of Maybach."<sup id="cite_ref-hertzner-pillsplug_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hertzner-pillsplug-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mercedes-Maybach">Mercedes-Maybach</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maybach&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Mercedes-Maybach">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Maybach_S600" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes-Maybach S600">Mercedes-Maybach S600</a></div>
<p>In November 2014, Daimler confirmed the revival of the Maybach brand as an ultra-luxury sub brand of the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_S-Class_(W222)" title="Mercedes-Benz S-Class (W222)">Mercedes-Benz S-Class (W222)</a>, positioned in luxury akin to the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_AMG" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes AMG">Mercedes AMG</a> sports brand. In anticipation of its April 2015 launch,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> the flagship Mercedes-Maybach S600 was unveiled at car shows in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, United States and <a href="/wiki/Guangzhou" title="Guangzhou">Guangzhou</a>, China,<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> and the production model at the 2015 <a href="/wiki/Geneva_Motor_Show" title="Geneva Motor Show">Geneva Motor Show</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Assembled on the same <a href="/wiki/Sindelfingen" title="Sindelfingen">Sindelfingen</a> line used for the S-Class, the model is designed to be pitched against the <a href="/wiki/Bentley_Flying_Spur" class="mw-redirect" title="Bentley Flying Spur">Bentley Flying Spur</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rolls-Royce_Ghost" title="Rolls-Royce Ghost">Rolls-Royce Ghost</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-AutocarSReview_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutocarSReview-22">[22]</a></sup> At 5.453 metres (17.89&#160;ft) long with a wheelbase of 3.365 metres (11.04&#160;ft) (132.5 inches), the new model is approximately 20 centimetres (7.9&#160;in) longer than the long-wheelbase S-Class models. The Mercedes-Maybach will be available as S500 (S550 in the US) and S600 models, with 4matic all-wheel-drive optional with the V8 engine.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Acceleration is 0 to 60 miles per hour (0 to 97&#160;km/h) in 5.0 seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-neil_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-neil-25">[25]</a></sup> The base car has several colour finish options and the choice between a three-seat rear bench, or two seats reclining. Options include: air-conditioned, heated and massaging seats; heated armrests; system to pump scented, <a href="/wiki/Ionised" class="mw-redirect" title="Ionised">ionised</a> air around the cabin; and a 1540 watt Burmester 3D surround sound system with 24 speakers.<sup id="cite_ref-AutocarSReview_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutocarSReview-22">[22]</a></sup> The Maybach S 500 assembly started in <a href="/wiki/Pune" title="Pune">Pune</a>, India in September 2015 and is the second country to produce a Maybach.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Performance">Performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maybach&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The Maybach 57 accelerates from 0 to 60&#160;mph (0 to 97&#160;km/h) in about 5.1 seconds; the Maybach 62 and 57 S, about 4.8 seconds; the Maybach 62 S and the Landaulet in 4.5 seconds. This rapid acceleration is noteworthy for cars weighing well over 6,000 pounds (2.7 metric tons). Maybachs in general are extremely powerful: the 57 has 518&#160;bhp (386&#160;kW; 525&#160;PS); the 57 S, 559&#160;bhp (417&#160;kW; 567&#160;PS); the 62, 570&#160;bhp (425&#160;kW; 578&#160;PS); the 62 S, 612&#160;bhp (456&#160;kW; 620&#160;PS), and the Landaulet, 633&#160;bhp (472&#160;kW; 642&#160;PS).</p>
