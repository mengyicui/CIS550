<p><b>Infiniti</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">インフィニティ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Infiniti</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, <small>IPA:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Japanese" title="Help:IPA for Japanese">[inɸinʲiti]</a></span>)</span> is the <a href="/wiki/Luxury_vehicle" title="Luxury vehicle">luxury vehicle</a> division of Japanese automaker <a href="/wiki/Nissan" title="Nissan">Nissan</a>. Infiniti officially started selling vehicles on November 8, 1989 in North America. The marketing network for Infiniti-branded vehicles now includes over 230 dealers in over 15 countries.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Infiniti marque is not used in Japan. However, the <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_Q50" title="Infiniti Q50">Infiniti Q50</a> is sold as a <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Skyline" title="Nissan Skyline">Nissan Skyline</a> in Japan and retains Infiniti badging, though without the Infiniti brand or name. Also, the original <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_Q45" title="Infiniti Q45">Q45</a> was sold as the <i>Nissan Infiniti Q45</i> in Japan from November 1989 until 1996.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Infiniti models have direct equivalents in the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_domestic_market" title="Japanese domestic market">Japanese domestic market</a> Nissan lineup. The <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_G" class="mw-redirect" title="Infiniti G">Infiniti G</a> as the <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Skyline" title="Nissan Skyline">Nissan Skyline</a> sedan and coupe and previously the <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Primera" title="Nissan Primera">Nissan Primera</a>, <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_M" title="Infiniti M">Infiniti M</a> as the <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Fuga" title="Nissan Fuga">Nissan Fuga</a> and previously the <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Leopard" title="Nissan Leopard">Nissan Leopard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_EX" title="Infiniti EX">Infiniti EX</a> as the <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Skyline" title="Nissan Skyline">Nissan Skyline</a> crossover, and <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_Q45" title="Infiniti Q45">Infiniti Q45</a> as the <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Cima" title="Nissan Cima">Nissan Cima</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_FX" class="mw-redirect" title="Infiniti FX">Infiniti FX</a> has no Nissan equivalent and is not sold in Japan.</p>
<p>In 2012, Infiniti moved its global headquarters from the Nissan corporate building in Yokohama and incorporated in <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a> as <b>Infiniti Global Limited</b>, with <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Ghosn" title="Carlos Ghosn">Carlos Ghosn</a> intending for Infiniti to have a greater focus on the burgeoning luxury market in mainland China as it forecast the country to become the largest luxury car market.<sup id="cite_ref-bloomberg013114_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloomberg013114-3">[3]</a></sup> Johan de Nysschen was appointed to head the Infiniti division in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> De Nysschen left the company as of August 2014 to become CEO of <a href="/wiki/Cadillac" title="Cadillac">Cadillac</a>. Infiniti Chairman <a href="/wiki/Andy_Palmer" title="Andy Palmer">Andy Palmer</a> assumed de Nysschen's responsibilities.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Nissan appointed Roland Krüger, former head of <a href="/wiki/BMW" title="BMW">BMW</a>'s Asian division, as president of Infiniti after the chairman of the marque, Andy Palmer, resigned to join <a href="/wiki/Aston_Martin" title="Aston Martin">Aston Martin</a> in September 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In mid-2013 Infiniti announced their Q30 Concept car would get its world premiere at the 2013 <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt_Motor_Show" class="mw-redirect" title="Frankfurt Motor Show">Frankfurt Motor Show</a>, a move which will see Infiniti start an aggressive <a href="/wiki/Strategy" title="Strategy">strategy</a> to extend the Infiniti brand into new <a href="/wiki/Premium_segment" title="Premium segment">premium</a> segments.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In 2013, Infiniti's model designation will change to the coupes and sedans starting with the letter Q (SUVs and crossovers will start with the letters QX) and a number reflecting the model's place in the brand lineup. For instance, the M sedan will become the Q70 while the G sedan will be replaced with the Q50; meanwhile, the QX56 will become the QX80 while the EX will become the QX50.<sup id="cite_ref-Korzeniewski_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Korzeniewski-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>With its <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_QX60" title="Infiniti QX60">QX60</a> crossover (formerly known as JX35) Infiniti began to produce vehicle outside Japan.<sup id="cite_ref-bloomberg013114_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloomberg013114-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2014, it is expected to start producing two models in <a href="/wiki/Xiangyang" title="Xiangyang">Xiangyang</a>, China, a plant operated by Nissan's joint venture with <a href="/wiki/Dongfeng_Motor" title="Dongfeng Motor">Dongfeng Motor</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bloomberg013114_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloomberg013114-3">[3]</a></sup> It is also expanding in Sunderland, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, to produce a new compact car named <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_Q30" title="Infiniti Q30">Q30</a> in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-bloomberg013114_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloomberg013114-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_beginning">The beginning</span></h3>

<p>The Infiniti brand was introduced in the United States in 1989. The <a href="/wiki/Marketing_strategy" title="Marketing strategy">marketing strategy</a> was to target the premium vehicle segments in the United States that would not have otherwise fit in with Nissan's more mainstream image, and partially influenced by the <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Accord" title="Plaza Accord">Plaza Accord</a> of 1985. The brand was created around the same time as Japanese rivals <a href="/wiki/Toyota" title="Toyota">Toyota</a> and <a href="/wiki/Honda" title="Honda">Honda</a> developed their <a href="/wiki/Lexus" title="Lexus">Lexus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Acura" title="Acura">Acura</a> premium brands. The <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Japan" title="Government of Japan">Japanese government</a> imposed <a href="/wiki/Voluntary_export_restraints" title="Voluntary export restraints">voluntary export restraints</a> for the U.S. market, so it was more profitable for automakers to export more expensive cars to the U.S.</p>
<p>The Infiniti marque was launched with two models, the <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_Q45" title="Infiniti Q45">Q45</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_M30" class="mw-redirect" title="Infiniti M30">M30</a> that were previously sold at Japanese <i><a href="/wiki/Nissan#Japan" title="Nissan">Nissan Motor Store</a></i> dealership networks. The Q45 was based on the all new second generation <a href="/wiki/Japanese_domestic_market" title="Japanese domestic market">JDM</a> <a href="/wiki/Nissan_President#President_HG50" title="Nissan President">Nissan President</a> on a five millimeter shorter wheelbase <a href="/wiki/Automobile_platform" class="mw-redirect" title="Automobile platform">platform</a> at 2,875&#160;mm (113.2&#160;in). Starting with model year 1992, the wheelbase matched the President's wheelbase at 2880&#160;mm (113.4&#160;in). The Q45 included a 278&#160;hp (207&#160;kW; 282&#160;PS) V8 engine, <a href="/wiki/Steering" title="Steering">four wheel steering</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Active_suspension" title="Active suspension">active suspension</a> system offered on the first generation Q45t. The car's features would have made it competitive in the full-sized "luxury" segment against the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_S-Class" title="Mercedes-Benz S-Class">Mercedes S-Class</a>, <a href="/wiki/BMW_7_Series" title="BMW 7 Series">BMW 7 Series</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_XJ" title="Jaguar XJ">Jaguar XJ</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cadillac_Fleetwood" title="Cadillac Fleetwood">Cadillac Fleetwood</a>.</p>

<p>A second model was introduced in 1990, the 2-door <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_M" title="Infiniti M">M30</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Badge_engineered" class="mw-redirect" title="Badge engineered">badge engineered</a> <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Leopard" title="Nissan Leopard">Nissan Leopard</a>. It remained in production for three years as an alternative to the <a href="/wiki/Lexus_SC" title="Lexus SC">Lexus SC</a>. The powertrain was the <a href="/wiki/VG30E" class="mw-redirect" title="VG30E">VG30E</a> 162&#160;hp (121&#160;kW; 164&#160;PS) engine and an automatic transmission. The M30 coupe was underpowered for its stock weight of 3,333&#160;lb (1,512&#160;kg). The M30 convertible weighed even more, due to the required body and chassis reinforcements. The appearance of the M30 had almost no resemblance to the larger Q45, and the interior was almost completely different.</p>
<p>Infiniti did not offer an <a href="/wiki/Executive_car" title="Executive car">mid-luxury</a> sedan to match the first Japanese luxury sedan introduced to North America, the <a href="/wiki/Acura_Legend" title="Acura Legend">Acura Legend</a>, which was later joined by the <a href="/wiki/Lexus_GS" title="Lexus GS">Lexus GS</a>. Infiniti's first offering in the entry-level luxury segment was the <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_J30" title="Infiniti J30">Infiniti J30</a>, which had to compete with the revised 1992 <a href="/wiki/Lexus_ES" title="Lexus ES">Lexus ES</a> and was unsuccessful owing to its small interior and unusual styling to which it was succeeded in 1996 by the <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_I" class="mw-redirect" title="Infiniti I">Infiniti I</a> series, related to the <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Maxima" title="Nissan Maxima">Nissan Maxima</a> and in 2002 by the <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_G35" class="mw-redirect" title="Infiniti G35">Infiniti G35</a>.</p>
<p>According to the company, the Infiniti badge has a double meaning, as stylized representations of both a road extending into the horizon and of <a href="/wiki/Mount_Fuji" title="Mount Fuji">Mount Fuji</a>, reflecting its Japanese origins.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<p>In 1991, Infiniti introduced a third model, the <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_G#First_generation_.28P10.2C_1990.E2.80.931996.29" class="mw-redirect" title="Infiniti G">Infiniti G20</a>, derived from the compact and European-focused <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Primera" title="Nissan Primera">Nissan Primera</a>.</p>
<p>For the 1993 model year, Infiniti introduced a <a href="/wiki/Coup%C3%A9#Four-door_coup.C3.A9" title="Coupé">four-door coupé</a> <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_J30" title="Infiniti J30">J30</a> with only one engine option, the 210&#160;hp (157&#160;kW; 213&#160;PS) <a href="/wiki/Nissan_VG_engine#VG30DE" title="Nissan VG engine">VG30DE</a>. This engine was from the 222&#160;hp (166&#160;kW; 225&#160;PS)<a href="/wiki/300ZX" class="mw-redirect" title="300ZX">300ZX</a>, the JDM <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Cedric" title="Nissan Cedric">Nissan Cedric</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Gloria" title="Nissan Gloria">Nissan Gloria</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Cima" title="Nissan Cima">Nissan Cima</a>, and was the North American version of the third generation JDM <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Leopard" title="Nissan Leopard">Nissan Leopard</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Infiniti sales were slow. The company's initial campaign aimed to bring about brand awareness with <a href="/wiki/Zen" title="Zen">Zen</a>-influenced spots that focused on nature and tranquility, without showing the actual cars.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Designers decided not to adorn the interiors with wood accents and chrome brightwork, opting instead for a <a href="/wiki/Monochrome" title="Monochrome">monochrome</a>, organic and rounded surface appearance, focusing on padded leather and vinyl throughout. Some buyers had faux wood appliques added to areas surrounding the center console and around the interior door handles. The only item that had a bright appearance was the centrally installed <a href="/wiki/Analog_clock" class="mw-redirect" title="Analog clock">analog clock</a> in all models, a design that is currently maintained by the designers.</p>
<p>By the mid-1990s, Infiniti was lagging behind <a href="/wiki/Lexus" title="Lexus">Lexus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Acura" title="Acura">Acura</a> in sales. The Q45 had retreated considerably from its focused, taut rendition of a sporty full-size luxury sedan, having become a barely recognizable, ponderously handling sedan that earned the nickname "The Japanese Lincoln". In 1999, Infiniti revived the <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_G20" class="mw-redirect" title="Infiniti G20">G20</a>, based on the discontinued JDM <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Primera" title="Nissan Primera">Nissan Primera</a>, a compact sport sedan whose production was halted for 1998 in Europe and Japan. The second generation G20 was marketed as a competitor to European entry-level luxury sport sedans, but it now weighed more than the first generation version. Because Infiniti continued to use the <a href="/wiki/SR20DE" class="mw-redirect" title="SR20DE">SR20DE</a> four-cylinder engine and compact size, it fell short of sales expectations. The G20 was also marketed primarily to the wrong demographic, that being middle-aged professional women, and as such it was not as popular new as its main competition, the similarly priced <a href="/wiki/Acura_Integra" class="mw-redirect" title="Acura Integra">Acura Integra</a>.</p>
<p>In 1997, Infiniti released the <a href="/wiki/QX4" class="mw-redirect" title="QX4">QX4</a>, modifying and adding premium accommodations to the <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Pathfinder" title="Nissan Pathfinder">Nissan Pathfinder</a>, becoming one of the first luxury car manufacturers to offer a mid-size premium SUV.</p>
<p>Infiniti replaced the J30 mid-sized rear drive sedan with the second-generation JDM <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Cefiro" title="Nissan Cefiro">Nissan Cefiro</a>, giving it the North American designation "I30" in 1996. It offered one engine, the 193&#160;PS (142&#160;kW; 190&#160;hp) <a href="/wiki/Nissan_VQ_engine#VQ30DE" title="Nissan VQ engine">VQ30DE</a> with front wheel drive shared with the Maxima. The I30 was redesigned for the 2000 model year, featuring an increase in power to 231 PS (170&#160;kW; 227&#160;hp). Infiniti made further changes to the I30's appearance and performance in 2002, upgrading the engine to the more powerful 259 PS (190&#160;kW; 255&#160;hp) <a href="/wiki/Nissan_VQ_engine#VQ35DE" title="Nissan VQ engine">VQ35DE</a>; this inspired a name change to the vehicle, which became the I35. Sales and the brand, bereft of an image or a following, floundered.</p>

<p>By 2000, large Japanese companies were feeling the effect of the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_asset_price_bubble" title="Japanese asset price bubble">Japanese asset price bubble</a>, and the reduced desirability of Infiniti's led to its facing extinction. The company rededicated itself to developing a dynamic and powerful line-up of sporty luxury cars. This coincided with parent company Nissan entering into an alliance with Renault under the direction of Carlos Ghosn and the <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Motor_Company#Alliance_with_Renault" class="mw-redirect" title="Nissan Motor Company">Nissan Revival Plan</a>. <i><a href="/wiki/Car_and_Driver" title="Car and Driver">Car and Driver</a></i> reported that Infiniti executives invited members of the motoring press to a meeting where they "swore never again to take their eyes off <a href="/wiki/BMW" title="BMW">BMW</a>."<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Although this effort began with a completely redesigned <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_Q45" title="Infiniti Q45">Q45</a> flagship for the 2002 model year, it was the <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_G" class="mw-redirect" title="Infiniti G">G35</a> that helped improve Infiniti sales in 2003. In Japan, Nissan had a well established reputation with premium level performance sedans after Nissan acquired the <a href="/wiki/Prince_Motor_Company" title="Prince Motor Company">Prince Motor Company</a> and integrated the <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Skyline" title="Nissan Skyline">Nissan Skyline</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Laurel" title="Nissan Laurel">Nissan Laurel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Gloria" title="Nissan Gloria">Nissan Gloria</a>, originally Prince vehicles, in 1966–1968. A sports sedan replacing its <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Primera" title="Nissan Primera">Nissan Primera</a>-based predecessor (the <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_G" class="mw-redirect" title="Infiniti G">G20</a>), the <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Skyline" title="Nissan Skyline">Nissan Skyline</a>-based Infiniti G35, was successful after it was voted <a href="/wiki/Motor_Trend_Car_of_the_Year" title="Motor Trend Car of the Year">Motor Trend Car of the Year</a> in 2003.</p>
<p>The release of the sport-tuned <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_FX" class="mw-redirect" title="Infiniti FX">FX35/45</a> <a href="/wiki/Crossover_(automobile)" title="Crossover (automobile)">crossover</a> that same year piggybacked on the G35's success. The FX used the same components as the G35 sport coupe and was designed for American tastes. It combined good handling and performance with <a href="/wiki/Station_wagon" title="Station wagon">station wagon</a>-like versatility and all-weather capability. In 2004, Infiniti added an all-wheel drive version of the G35 sports sedan to compete with similar all-wheel drive sports sedans from <a href="/wiki/Audi" title="Audi">Audi</a> and <a href="/wiki/BMW" title="BMW">BMW</a>. Infiniti also introduced a larger SUV to compete with the <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Land_Cruiser" title="Toyota Land Cruiser">Toyota Land Cruiser</a>/Lexus LX470. This was the 2004 <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_QX#First_generation_series_JA60_.282004.E2.80.932010.29" class="mw-redirect" title="Infiniti QX">Infiniti QX56</a> based on the U.S. market <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Armada" title="Nissan Armada">Nissan Armada</a>.</p>
<p>Nissan Motors President and CEO, <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Ghosn" title="Carlos Ghosn">Carlos Ghosn</a>, developed a <a href="/wiki/Strategic_management" title="Strategic management">business strategy</a> to break Infiniti away from its Nissan roots. The G series helped re-define Infiniti as the "Japanese BMW", as it was aimed directly at the BMW 3 Series of sedans and coupes.<a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://www.theglobeandmail.com/globe-drive/new-cars/auto-news/luxury-car-shopper-should-consider-a-japanese-bmw/article5763577/">[1]</a> The M35/M45 garnered acclaim from the automotive press, winning an eight-car comparison test in <i><a href="/wiki/Car_and_Driver" title="Car and Driver">Car and Driver</a></i>, and the model was named the best luxury sedan by <i><a href="/wiki/Consumer_Reports" title="Consumer Reports">Consumer Reports</a></i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The M45 model featured the same engine as the Q45, and the M45 became the flagship model after the Q45 was discontinued. The Infiniti M (2003–2005) and the Infiniti G (starting in 2003) were known in Japan as the Nissan Gloria and the Nissan Skyline.</p>
<p>For the 2007 model year a redesigned version of the G35 sedan was introduced, followed in 2008 by a new version of the company's G coupe, the G37. The coupe was first unveiled at the <a href="/wiki/New_York_International_Auto_Show" title="New York International Auto Show">New York International Auto Show</a>. Also released in 2008 was new <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_EX35" class="mw-redirect" title="Infiniti EX35">Infiniti EX35</a> compact crossover, Infiniti's entry into the compact luxury crossover market. The EX shared the G35's 3.5-liter, 306&#160;hp (228&#160;kW), V6 engine.</p>
<p>Carlos Ghosn unveiled Infiniti’s arrival in Europe at the Geneva Motor Show 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The official launch was late 2008, and was phased over a two-year period across 21 European countries. Four models are offered in Europe: the next generation of the <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_FX" class="mw-redirect" title="Infiniti FX">Infiniti FX</a>37 and <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_FX" class="mw-redirect" title="Infiniti FX">Infiniti FX</a>50 performance SUV, the <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_G37" class="mw-redirect" title="Infiniti G37">Infiniti G37</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_G37" class="mw-redirect" title="Infiniti G37">Infiniti G37</a> coupe, and the <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_EX" title="Infiniti EX">Infiniti EX</a>37 crossover. Infiniti Europe’s headquarters are based in Rolle, Switzerland.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<p>As of 2011, the <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_G" class="mw-redirect" title="Infiniti G">G</a> Series (consisting of the sedan, coupe, and convertible) accounts for 60% of sales. In 2011, Infiniti sales fell, while other import luxury vehicles were doing well.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Furthermore, Infiniti's performance over practicality emphasis has resulted in its crossovers and SUVs faring poorly among women compared to offerings from Acura and Lexus.<sup id="cite_ref-Monticello_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Monticello-18">[18]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Models">Models</span></h2>
<p>Infiniti's former model name designation included one letter for <a href="/wiki/Coupe" class="mw-redirect" title="Coupe">coupés</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sedan_(car)" class="mw-redirect" title="Sedan (car)">sedans</a> (two letters for <a href="/wiki/SUV" class="mw-redirect" title="SUV">SUVs</a>) and a number reflecting engine displacement. For example, the <b><a href="/wiki/Infiniti_QX56" title="Infiniti QX56">QX56</a></b> was an SUV featuring a 5.6&#160;L engine. An exception to this was the <b><a href="/wiki/Infiniti_QX4" class="mw-redirect" title="Infiniti QX4">QX4</a></b> SUV, which featured a 3.3&#160;L engine (1997–2000) and later a 3.5&#160;L engine (2001–2003). An "<b>x</b>" following the engine displacement of Infiniti sedans denoted an <a href="/wiki/All_wheel_drive" class="mw-redirect" title="All wheel drive">all wheel drive</a> model (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_G35x" class="mw-redirect" title="Infiniti G35x">Infiniti G35x</a>), "<b>s</b>" denoted a sport package, "<b>h</b>" denoted a hybrid model, "<b>d</b>" denoted a diesel model (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_M#M30d_.28Diesel_version.29_.282011.2B.29" title="Infiniti M">M30d</a>) and a "<b>t</b>" for a Touring model (older models only).</p>
<p>In 2013, Infiniti's model designation changed to the coupes and sedans starting with the letter Q, the SUVs and crossovers starting with the letters QX, and a number reflecting the model's place in the brand lineup. For instance, the M sedan became the Q70 while the G sedan was replaced with the Q50; meanwhile, the QX56 became the QX80 while the EX became the QX50.<sup id="cite_ref-Korzeniewski_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Korzeniewski-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
