<p><b>Hyundai Group</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>:&#160;<span lang="ko-Hang"><span lang="ko">현대그룹</span></span>; <a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">Hanja</a>:&#160;<span lang="ko-Hani"><span lang="ko">現代그룹</span></span>; <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'h' in 'hi'">h</span><span title="/j/ 'y' in 'yes'">j</span><span title="/ʌ/ short 'u' in 'bud'">ʌ</span><span title="'n' in 'no'">n</span><span title="'d' in 'dye'">d</span><span title="/ɛ/ short 'e' in 'bed'">ɛ</span></span>/</a></span></span>)<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporation" title="Multinational corporation">multinational</a> <a href="/wiki/Chaebol" title="Chaebol">chaebol</a> (conglomerate) headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>. It was founded by <a href="/wiki/Chung_Ju-yung" title="Chung Ju-yung">Chung Ju-yung</a> in 1947 as a <a href="/wiki/Construction" title="Construction">construction</a> firm and Chung was directly in control of the company until his death in 2001.</p>
<p>Following the <a href="/wiki/1997_East_Asian_financial_crisis" class="mw-redirect" title="1997 East Asian financial crisis">1997 East Asian financial crisis</a> and Chung's death, Hyundai underwent a major restructuring and break-up, which reduced the <a href="/wiki/Hyundai_Group" title="Hyundai Group">Hyundai Group</a>'s business to encompass only container shipping services, the manufacturing of elevators, and tourism. Today, most companies bearing the name Hyundai are not legally connected to Hyundai Group. They include <a href="/wiki/Hyundai_Motor_Group" title="Hyundai Motor Group">Hyundai Motor Group</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hyundai_Department_Store_Group" title="Hyundai Department Store Group">Hyundai Department Store Group</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hyundai_Heavy_Industries_Group" title="Hyundai Heavy Industries Group">Hyundai Heavy Industries Group</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hyundai_Development_Company" title="Hyundai Development Company">Hyundai Development Company</a>. However, most of the former subsidiaries of the Hyundai conglomerate continue to be run by relatives of Chung. If these companies were considered as forming a single broad family business, then it would remain the largest company in South Korea with enormous economic and political power in the country.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hyundai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Hyundai" comes from the Korean word 現代 (<a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">hanja</a> form), which means "modernity".<sup id="cite_ref-smokescompetition_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-smokescompetition-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hyundai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hyundai was founded as a small construction firm by <a href="/wiki/Chung_Ju-yung" title="Chung Ju-yung">Chung Ju-yung</a> in 1947.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Hyundai Construction began operating outside of South Korea in 1965, initially entering the markets of Guam, Thailand and Vietnam.<sup id="cite_ref-changeface_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-changeface-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hyundai Motor Company was founded in 1967.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Hyundai Heavy Industries was founded in 1973,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and completed the construction of its first ships in June 1974.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1983 Hyundai entered the semiconductor industry through the establishment of Hyundai Electronics (renamed <a href="/wiki/Hynix" class="mw-redirect" title="Hynix">Hynix</a> in 2001).<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1986 a Hyundai-manufactured <a href="/wiki/IBM_PC_compatible" title="IBM PC compatible">IBM PC-XT compatible</a> called the <b>Blue Chip PC</b> was sold in discount and toy stores throughout the US. It was one of the earliest PC clones marketed toward consumers instead of business.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hyundai announced a major management restructuring in December 1995, affecting 404 executives.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In April 1999 Hyundai announced a major corporate restructuring, involving a two-thirds reduction of the number of business units and a plan to break up the group into five independent business groups by 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<p>By the mid-1990s Hyundai comprised over 60 subsidiary companies and was active in a diverse range of activities including automobile manufacturing, construction, chemicals, electronics, financial services, heavy industry and shipbuilding.<sup id="cite_ref-changeface_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-changeface-4">[4]</a></sup> In the same period it had total annual revenues of around US$90 billion and over 200,000 employees.<sup id="cite_ref-changeface_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-changeface-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hyundai_Motor_Company">Hyundai Motor Company</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hyundai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Hyundai Motor Company">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Hyundai_Motor_Company" title="Hyundai Motor Company">Hyundai Motor Company</a></div>
<p>Hyundai branded vehicles are manufactured by Hyundai Motor Company, which along with <a href="/wiki/Kia_Motors" title="Kia Motors">Kia</a> comprises the <a href="/wiki/Hyundai_Motor_Group" title="Hyundai Motor Group">Hyundai Kia Automotive Group</a>. Headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>, Hyundai operates the world's largest integrated <a href="/wiki/Automobile" class="mw-redirect" title="Automobile">automobile</a> manufacturing facility<sup id="cite_ref-smokescompetition_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-smokescompetition-2">[2]</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Ulsan" title="Ulsan">Ulsan</a>, which is capable of producing 1.6 million units annually. The company employs about 75,000 people around the world. Hyundai vehicles are sold in 193 countries through some 6,000 <a href="/wiki/Car_dealership" title="Car dealership">dealerships</a> and <a href="/wiki/Showroom" title="Showroom">showrooms</a> worldwide. In 2012, Hyundai sold over 4.4 million vehicles worldwide. Popular models include the <a href="/wiki/Hyundai_Sonata" title="Hyundai Sonata">Sonata</a> midsize sedan and <a href="/wiki/Hyundai_Elantra" title="Hyundai Elantra">Elantra</a> compact.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Asan Foundation, established by <a href="/wiki/Chung_Ju-yung" title="Chung Ju-yung">Chung Ju-yung</a> in 1977 with 50 percent of the stock of <a href="/wiki/Hyundai_Engineering_%26_Construction" title="Hyundai Engineering &amp; Construction">Hyundai Construction</a>, subsidizes medical services in Korea primarily through the <a href="/wiki/Asan_Medical_Center" title="Asan Medical Center">Asan Medical Center</a> and six other hospitals. The foundation has sponsored conferences on Eastern ethics and funded academic research into traditional Korean culture. In 1991, it established the annual <a href="/wiki/Filial_piety" title="Filial piety">Filial Piety</a> Award.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
