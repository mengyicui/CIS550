<p><b>Hummer</b> was a brand of trucks and <a href="/wiki/SUV" class="mw-redirect" title="SUV">SUVs</a>, first marketed in 1992 when <a href="/wiki/AM_General" title="AM General">AM General</a> began selling a civilian version of the M998&#160;<a href="/wiki/Humvee" title="Humvee">Humvee</a>. In 1998, <a href="/wiki/General_Motors" title="General Motors">General Motors</a> (GM) purchased the brand name and marketed three vehicles: the original <a href="/wiki/Hummer_H1" title="Hummer H1">Hummer&#160;H1</a>, based on the military Humvee, as well as the <a href="/wiki/Hummer_H2" title="Hummer H2">H2</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hummer_H3" title="Hummer H3">H3</a> models that were based on smaller, civilian-market GM <a href="/wiki/Automobile_platform" class="mw-redirect" title="Automobile platform">platforms</a>.</p>
<p>By 2008, Hummer's viability in the <a href="/wiki/Late-2000s_recession" class="mw-redirect" title="Late-2000s recession">economic downturn</a> was being questioned, and it was placed under review by GM management. Rather than being transferred to the <a href="/wiki/Motors_Liquidation_Company" title="Motors Liquidation Company">Motors Liquidation Company</a> as part of the GM bankruptcy in 2009, the brand was retained by GM, in order to investigate its sale.</p>
<p>In 2009, a Chinese manufacturer, <a href="/wiki/Tengzhong" title="Tengzhong">Sichuan Tengzhong Heavy Industrial Machinery Company</a>, announced that it would acquire Hummer, pending government approvals, but later withdrew its bid.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> On February 24, 2010, Reuters reported that the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Commerce_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="Ministry of Commerce of the People's Republic of China">Chinese ministry of commerce</a> had prevented the deal,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> although a ministry spokesperson denied rejecting the application, which had been stalled for eight months.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> At the end of February, General Motors announced it would begin dismantling the Hummer brand.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Although the automaker announced two days later that it had been approached with new offers,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> by April 2010, any sale became unlikely, as inventory was depleted and Hummer dealerships began shutting down.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> After filling a rental-car fleet order, the last Hummer&#160;H3 rolled off the line at <a href="/wiki/Shreveport" class="mw-redirect" title="Shreveport">Shreveport</a> on May 24, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The original maker of Hummer, AM General, lost their bid to build the <a href="/wiki/Humvee_replacement_process" title="Humvee replacement process">HMMWV's replacement</a> for the U.S. military in 2015.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hummer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Origin">Origin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hummer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Origin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The original Hummers were first designed by <a href="/wiki/AM_General" title="AM General">AM General</a> Corporation, a wholly owned subsidiary <a href="/wiki/American_Motors" title="American Motors">American Motors Corporation</a> (AMC), and built in its <a href="/wiki/Mishawaka,_Indiana" title="Mishawaka, Indiana">Mishawaka, Indiana</a>, assembly plant.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In 1979, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">United States Army</a> was seeking contractors for a new "High Mobility Multi-Purpose Wheeled Vehicle" which could follow the tracks and ruts of full size army trucks (HMMWV).<sup id="cite_ref-historyhistory_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-historyhistory-9">[9]</a></sup> Among the four competitors for the contract, AM General designed an entirely new vehicle to meet the Army's requirements.<sup id="cite_ref-historyhistory_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-historyhistory-9">[9]</a></sup> In less than one year, it was the first to deliver a prototype vehicle. Initial production versions were delivered to the Army's proving grounds in April 1982.<sup id="cite_ref-historyhistory_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-historyhistory-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>After testing was completed AM General was awarded the contract to supply its HMMWV to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_armed_forces" class="mw-redirect" title="United States armed forces">United States armed forces</a>. The first models were built in a variety of military-based equipment and versions. The first contract was in 1983, worth US$1.2 billion to produce 55,000 "Humvees" by 1985.<sup id="cite_ref-corphistory_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-corphistory-10">[10]</a></sup> The first production vehicle was assembled by AM General on January 2, 1985.<sup id="cite_ref-historyhistory_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-historyhistory-9">[9]</a></sup> The contract was later increased for an additional 15,000 units.<sup id="cite_ref-historyhistory_9-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-historyhistory-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>AM General had planned to sell a civilian version of the Humvee as far back as the late-1980s. Having the same structure and most mechanical components, the civilian Hummers were finished in automotive gloss paint, adding passenger car enhancements such as <a href="/wiki/Air_conditioning" title="Air conditioning">air conditioning</a>, sound insulation, upgraded upholstery, stereo systems, wood trim, and convenience packages.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The civilian model began in part because of the persistence of <a href="/wiki/Arnold_Schwarzenegger" title="Arnold Schwarzenegger">Arnold Schwarzenegger</a>, who saw an Army convoy while filming a movie.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1992, AM General began selling a civilian version of the M998 <a href="/wiki/Humvee" title="Humvee">Humvee</a> vehicle to the public under the brand name "Hummer".<sup id="cite_ref-corphistory_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-corphistory-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="GM_purchase">GM purchase</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hummer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: GM purchase">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In December 1999, AM General sold the brand name to General Motors, but continued to manufacture the vehicles.<sup id="cite_ref-corphistory_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-corphistory-10">[10]</a></sup> GM was responsible for the marketing and distribution of all Hummers produced by AM General. Shortly thereafter, GM introduced two of its own design models, the <a href="/wiki/Hummer_H2" title="Hummer H2">H2</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hummer_H3" title="Hummer H3">H3</a>, and renamed the original vehicle H1. AM General continued to build the H1 until it was discontinued in 2006,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and was contracted by GM to produce the H2. The H3 was built in Shreveport, LA alongside the <a href="/wiki/Chevrolet_Colorado" title="Chevrolet Colorado">Chevrolet Colorado</a> and GMC Canyon pickups, with which it shared the <a href="/wiki/GM_GMT_platform#GMT_355" title="GM GMT platform">GMT-355</a> platform (modified and designated GMT-345). Hummer dealership buildings featured an oversized half <a href="/wiki/Quonset_Hut" class="mw-redirect" title="Quonset Hut">Quonset Hut</a> style roof, themed to the Hummer brand's military origins.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>By 2006, the Hummer began to be exported and sold through importers and distributors in 33 countries.<sup id="cite_ref-gmpr06_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gmpr06-15">[15]</a></sup> On October 10, 2006, GM began producing the Hummer H3 at its <a href="/wiki/Port_Elizabeth" title="Port Elizabeth">Port Elizabeth</a> plant in South Africa for international markets.<sup id="cite_ref-gmpr06_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gmpr06-15">[15]</a></sup> The Hummers built there at first were only left-hand drive, but right-hand drive versions were added and exported to Australia and other markets.</p>
<p>The H2 was also assembled in <a href="/wiki/Kaliningrad" title="Kaliningrad">Kaliningrad</a>, Russia, by <a href="/wiki/Avtotor" title="Avtotor">Avtotor</a>, starting in 2006 and ending in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The plant produced a few hundred vehicles annually, and its output was limited to local consumption with five dealers in Russia.</p>
<p>On June 3, 2008, one day prior to GM's annual shareholder meeting, Rick Wagoner, GM's CEO at that time, said the brand was being reviewed, and had the possibility of either being sold, having the production line completely redesigned, or being discontinued.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> This was due to the decreasing demand for large SUVs as a result of higher oil prices. Almost immediately after the announcement, a pair of Indian automakers, including <a href="/wiki/Mahindra_%26_Mahindra" title="Mahindra &amp; Mahindra">Mahindra &amp; Mahindra</a>, expressed interest in purchasing all or part of Hummer.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>In April 2009, GM President <a href="/wiki/Fritz_Henderson" class="mw-redirect" title="Fritz Henderson">Fritz Henderson</a> stated several interested parties had approached GM regarding the Hummer business.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Failed_sale">Failed sale</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hummer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Failed sale">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On June 1, 2009, as a part of the <a href="/wiki/General_Motors_bankruptcy" class="mw-redirect" title="General Motors bankruptcy">General Motors bankruptcy</a> announcement, the company revealed that the Hummer brand would be discontinued. However, the following day GM announced that instead it had reached a deal to sell the brand to an undisclosed buyer.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT-june3_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-june3-20">[20]</a></sup> After GM announced that same day that the sale was to an undisclosed Chinese company,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> CNN and the New York Times identified the buyer of the Hummer truck unit as China-based <a href="/wiki/Sichuan_Tengzhong_Heavy_Industrial_Machinery_Company" class="mw-redirect" title="Sichuan Tengzhong Heavy Industrial Machinery Company">Sichuan Tengzhong Heavy Industrial Machinery Company</a> Ltd.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT-june3_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-june3-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Later that day, Sichuan Tengzhong itself announced the deal on their own website.</p>
<p>On January 6, 2010, GM CEO <a href="/wiki/Ed_Whitacre" class="mw-redirect" title="Ed Whitacre">Ed Whitacre</a> said he hoped to close the deal with Tengzhong by the end of that month.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> On February 1, 2010, it was announced that Sichuan and General Motors had agreed to extend the deadline until the end of February as Sichuan tried to get approval by the Chinese government.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> It was also revealed that the price tag of the Hummer brand was $150 million.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>Later, on February 24, 2010, GM announced the Tengzhong deal had collapsed and the Hummer brand would soon shut down.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> There were reports that Sichuan Tengzhong might pursue the purchase of the Hummer brand from GM by purchasing it privately through the company's new J&amp;A Tengzhong Fund SPC, a private equity investment fund owned by an offshore entity that was recruiting private investors to buy into its acquisition plan.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> The financial markets posed problems for established borrowers and even more for Tengzhong, a little-known company from western China, at the same time as the potential value of the Hummer brand continued to decline given high fuel prices and weak consumer demand.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>The company announced it was willing to consider offers for all or part of the assets. American company Raser Technologies along with several others expressed interest in buying the company. However, on April 7, 2010, this attempt failed as well, and General Motors officially said it was shutting down the Hummer SUV brand and offering rich rebates in a bid to move the remaining 2,200 vehicles.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hummer_H1">Hummer H1</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hummer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Hummer H1">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Hummer_H1" title="Hummer H1">Hummer H1</a></div>
<p>The first vehicle in the Hummer range was the <a href="/wiki/Hummer_H1" title="Hummer H1">Hummer H1</a>, based on the <a href="/wiki/Humvee" title="Humvee">Humvee</a>. Originally released in 1992, this vehicle was designed by <a href="/wiki/American_Motors" title="American Motors">American Motors</a>' AM General subsidiary for the U.S. Military. Five years previously, AMC had been bought by <a href="/wiki/Chrysler" title="Chrysler">Chrysler</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hummer_H2">Hummer H2</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hummer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Hummer H2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Hummer_H2" title="Hummer H2">Hummer H2</a></div>
<p>The Hummer H2 was the second vehicle in the Hummer range. There were two variations: The H2 SUV and H2 SUT.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hummer_H3">Hummer H3</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hummer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Hummer H3">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Hummer_H3" title="Hummer H3">Hummer H3</a></div>
<p>The H3 and H3T truck were the smallest of the Hummer models and were based on the <a href="/wiki/GM_GMT_platform#GMT_355" title="GM GMT platform">GMT355 platform</a> shared with the <a href="/wiki/Chevrolet_Colorado" title="Chevrolet Colorado">Chevrolet Colorado</a> and <a href="/wiki/GMC_Canyon" class="mw-redirect" title="GMC Canyon">GMC Canyon</a> compact pickup trucks.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hummer_HX">Hummer HX</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hummer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Hummer HX">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Hummer_HX" title="Hummer HX">Hummer HX</a></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Hummer_HX" title="Hummer HX">Hummer HX</a> was developed in 2008 as an open-air, two-door off-road <a href="/wiki/Concept_car" title="Concept car">concept car</a>, smaller than other Hummer models.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Plug-in_hybrid">Plug-in hybrid</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hummer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Plug-in hybrid">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Raser Technologies (formerly of Utah)<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> was to use technology similar to that in the <a href="/wiki/Chevrolet_Volt" title="Chevrolet Volt">Chevrolet Volt</a>. The company unveiled the prototype to the 2009 Society of Automotive Engineers World Congress in Detroit.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> The E-REV (Extended-Range Electric Vehicle) powertrain technology, was claimed to power the vehicle for up to 40 miles (64&#160;km) on its battery, and then a small 4-cylinder internal combustion engine would start to generate more electricity.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>

<p>Team Hummer Racing was created in 1993. Led by off-road racer <a href="/wiki/Rod_Hall_(racer)" title="Rod Hall (racer)">Rod Hall</a>, Team Hummer competed in the stock classes of both BitD and SCORE, with specialized racing shock absorbers, tires, and other modifications, along with mandatory safety equipment. Team Hummer earned 11 production-class wins at the Baja 1000.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticisms">Criticisms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hummer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Criticisms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Criticism of Hummers mirrors the <a href="/wiki/Criticism_of_sport_utility_vehicles" title="Criticism of sport utility vehicles">criticism of SUVs in general</a>, but to a higher degree. Specific criticisms of Hummers include:</p>
<dl>
<dt>Size</dt>
<dd>Hummers (specifically the H1 and H2) are significantly bigger than other SUVs; this can cause problems parking, driving and fitting in a garage. Their large size may also pose a serious threat to smaller vehicles.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Poor fuel economy</dt>
<dd>Even compared to other heavy passenger vehicles, Hummers without the diesel engine options have very poor <a href="/wiki/Fuel_economy_in_automobiles" title="Fuel economy in automobiles">fuel economy</a>. Because the H2 is built to the over-8500-lb GVW class, its fuel economy is neither published by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Environmental_Protection_Agency" title="United States Environmental Protection Agency">U.S. EPA</a> nor counted toward <a href="/wiki/Corporate_Average_Fuel_Economy" title="Corporate Average Fuel Economy">Corporate Average Fuel Economy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-MT-august02_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MT-august02-37">[37]</a></sup> For example, H2 in one engine configuration averages an estimated 14&#160;mpg<sub>US</sub> (17&#160;L/100&#160;km; 17&#160;mpg<sub>imp</sub>) on the highway and 10&#160;mpg<sub>US</sub> (24&#160;L/100&#160;km; 12&#160;mpg<sub>imp</sub>) in the city.<sup id="cite_ref-MT-august02_37-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MT-august02-37">[37]</a></sup> It has a <a href="/wiki/Curb_weight" title="Curb weight">curb weight</a> of around 6,400&#160;lb (2,900&#160;kg).<sup id="cite_ref-MT-august02_37-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MT-august02-37">[37]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Safety</dt>
<dd>Crash data for Hummers is less complete than for other SUVs. As a Class 3 truck, the Hummer is exempt from many DOT safety regulations.<sup id="cite_ref-humhis4_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-humhis4-38">[38]</a></sup> The H1 lacks standard safety features, including child safety locks, child seat tethers, side air bags, and stability control. Large <a href="/wiki/Blind_spot_(vehicle)" class="mw-redirect" title="Blind spot (vehicle)">blind spots</a> make parking difficult and possibly dangerous.<sup id="cite_ref-humhis4_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-humhis4-38">[38]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Drivers</dt>
<dd>A one-year study, conducted by a firm that provides statistical information to insurance companies, found that drivers of H2 and H3 Hummers receive about five times as many <a href="/wiki/Traffic_tickets" class="mw-redirect" title="Traffic tickets">traffic tickets</a> as the national average for all vehicles (standardized based on the number of violations per 100,000 miles driven).<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Licensing">Licensing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hummer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Licensing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
